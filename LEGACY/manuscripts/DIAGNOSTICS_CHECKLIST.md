# Diagnostics checklist — rat-containerization

This checklist documents which assumptions of the staggered-DiD
identification strategy were tested, where the results live, and how
to interpret each pass/fail. The live numbers come from
`artifacts/diagnostic_checklist.parquet` (regenerated by
`06_synthesis_and_publication.py`) and the JSON artifacts produced by
notebooks 02 + 04 + 05 + 07 + 08.

| # | Diagnostic | Test | Notebook | Status interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pre-treatment balance** | Standardized mean differences (SMD) per covariate, treated vs. control | `02_balance_and_pretrends.py` | `\|SMD\| < 0.5` acceptable; `< 0.1` ideal. Severe imbalance flags omitted-variable concerns. |
| 2 | **Parallel trends (visual)** | Group-mean line plot, treated + control through 2024 with pilot date marked | `02_balance_and_pretrends.py` (figure) | Lines should track in parallel pre-June 2024; visible divergence post-June is the treatment effect. |
| 3 | **Multi-estimator agreement** | TWFE + Callaway-Sant'Anna + synthetic control side-by-side | `03_main_effects.py` | Convergence across estimators is the strongest robustness check. Divergence triggers `mechanism` analysis (notebook 05). |
| 4 | **Residual normality** | Jarque-Bera + Shapiro-Wilk on TWFE residuals | `04_diagnostics.py` | `p > 0.05` supports normal residuals. Failure suggests non-Gaussian errors; clustered SE is robust to this but interpretation should note. |
| 5 | **Heteroskedasticity (visual)** | Residuals-vs-fitted + Q-Q | `04_diagnostics.py` (figure) | Q-Q deviation in tails or fan-shaped residuals → cluster-robust SE preferred. |
| 6 | **Leave-one-treated-out jackknife** | Drop each treated CD; refit; report ATT range | `04_diagnostics.py` | Tight range (`max - min` small) means no single unit drives the ATT. Wide range → influential-unit problem. |
| 7 | **Block bootstrap CI** | Resample districts (B=200), refit TWFE, percentile CI | `04_diagnostics.py` | Bootstrap CI close to analytic CI = good agreement. Bootstrap CI much wider → analytic SE understates uncertainty. |
| 8 | **HTE by baseline volume** | Stratify by pre-treatment median; refit per subgroup | `05_robustness_and_mechanism.py` | Subgroup ATTs in same direction → average effect interpretation OK. Diverging signs → policy effect is heterogeneous. |
| 9 | **Placebo test (within-2024)** | Treatment shifted to Jan 2024, restricted to pre-true-treatment data | `05_robustness_and_mechanism.py` | `p > 0.05` on placebo ATT = parallel trends hold. `p < 0.05` = pre-treatment differential trend. |

| 12 | **McCrary-style density continuity** | Chi-square test on density of CDs in [-5, 0] vs [0, +5] km of cutoff | `07_rdd_and_spatial.py` | `p > 0.05` → density continuous (no manipulation). **Note:** in our setting "manipulation" doesn't make literal sense — the CDs are fixed. | 14 | **Power analysis (MDE)** | `nyc311.stats.minimum_detectable_effect` swept over ICC × R² grid | `08_extended_robustness.py` | Compare MDE to observed TWFE ATT. If `\|ATT\| < MDE` the design is underpowered to declare significance even if the true effect is exactly the observed one. |

## What this showcase actually finds

Reading the artifacts (numbers will update as data refreshes):

- The **multi-year pre-trend test (#15)** is marginally **failing**
  (p ≈ 0.05) — treated CDs had a slight downward trend in 2022–May 2024
  relative to controls, which biases the post-treatment ATT upward.
  This is a real concern that the upstream analysis missed.
- **Spatial spillover (#13)** is strongly significant (ρ ≈ 0.52,
  p < 0.001) and the SAR-corrected treatment coefficient is **negative
  and substantively larger in magnitude** than the naive TWFE (which
  was +6.8). Strong evidence that spillover is masking the true effect.
- **MDE (#14)** at default ICC=0.05 with no covariates is **~21
  complaints** — well above the observed |ATT| ≈ 7. The design is
  underpowered to declare any of the observed effects significant.
- **Reporting-bias EM (#16)** still collapses to uniform even with 36
  months + ACS covariates. The model is genuinely underdetermined for
  this kind of data; needs media-coverage or DOH-inspection
  instruments to identify.
- **McCrary (#12)** **fails** as expected — the running variable has a
  geographic discontinuity in CD density at the cutoff (the treated
  zone is on a peninsula), which limits RDD's identifying assumption.
  Documented as a known limitation rather than a real manipulation
  finding.
- **BH correction (#17)** shrinks 2 raw-significant tests to 1
  family-wise significant after FDR control — small but illustrative.

## What's still NOT tested (next iteration)

- **Record-level RDD with `rdrobust`** — local-polynomial with
  IK-optimal bandwidth selection at the individual-complaint level
  (using lat/lon from the raw CSV records, not CD centroids). Would
  give a much larger sample and more granular running variable.
- **Synthetic control inference** — placebo permutation test
  (Abadie-Diamond-Hainmueller p-values via cross-validation against
  donor pool).
- **Event-study with full pre-period leads** — formal estimation of
  pre-treatment leads using the multi-year panel + visualization of
  coefficients with pointwise + uniform CIs.
- **Heterogeneous spillover** — does spillover concentrate in
  immediately-adjacent CDs vs. the whole 3 km neighborhood?
- **DiD with covariates** — current TWFE includes only treatment +
  unit + time FE; adding ACS demographics as time-invariant controls
  would reduce residual variance.
